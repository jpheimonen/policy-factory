# 005: Heartbeat, classifier, and idea prompts

## Context

This step completes the prompt overhaul by rewriting the remaining 6 prompt files: three heartbeat prompts (skim, triage, sa-update), the classifier prompt, and two idea prompts (evaluate, generate). This step also adds the final validation tests confirming zero "tech policy" references remain across all prompt files in the system.

The heartbeat prompts are the most impactful changes in this step. The skim prompt's escalation threshold is too low — noise gets through while genuinely significant events are missed. The significance criteria are all tech-focused ("Change Finland's geopolitical technology position," "Affect EU technology regulation"). The SA update prompt produces bureaucratic corporatespeak despite containing the instruction "Be factual." These prompts are the system's first line of contact with real-world events and directly determine the quality of ongoing situational awareness.

The classifier and idea prompts have the same tech-policy scoping problem as everything else, but their core logic is sound and just needs broadening.

## Deliverables

### 1. Rewrite `src/policy_factory/prompts/heartbeat/skim.md`

Two major changes:

**Scope broadening:** Replace the 6 tech-specific significance criteria with general policy significance criteria. A development is significant if it could:
- Shift Finland's geopolitical position or alliance relationships
- Trigger a major policy response from the Finnish government or parliament
- Alter Finland's security environment (military, energy, border, cyber)
- Significantly affect Finland's economic structure or trade relationships
- Change the immigration, demographic, or social cohesion landscape
- Impact EU integration dynamics or Finland's position within the EU
- Invalidate a current assumption in the situational awareness layer

**Raised escalation threshold:** The current threshold is too low — routine news gets through. The new threshold must be significantly higher. Add explicit guidance on what should NOT escalate:
- Routine government announcements and press releases
- Incremental regulatory updates without strategic implications
- Minor corporate news, earnings reports, product launches
- Local incidents without systemic implications
- Developments already well-covered in the current SA layer

And what SHOULD escalate:
- Major geopolitical shifts (alliance changes, military actions, sanctions, territorial changes)
- Significant military or security developments affecting Finland or the Baltic/Nordic region
- Major policy changes by key partners or adversaries (US, EU, Russia, China)
- Events that invalidate current strategic assumptions
- Developments that would change a policy recommendation if policymakers knew about them

Preserve template variables `{current_date}`, `{sa_summary}`, `{news_headlines}`. Preserve the exact output format markers `STATUS: NOTHING_NOTEWORTHY` and `STATUS: FLAGGED` — the orchestrator parses these strings.

### 2. Rewrite `src/policy_factory/prompts/heartbeat/triage.md`

Replace "tech policy analysis system" with general policy framing. The existing threshold text ("would this change a policy recommendation if policymakers knew about it?") is the right threshold and should be kept. Changes:

- Broaden the assessment criteria from tech-specific to general policy scope
- Reinforce the selectivity instruction — the triage agent should be a hard filter, not a rubber stamp
- Add instruction to assess whether the flagged item is genuinely new information or just restating something already in the SA layer with different phrasing

Preserve template variables `{flagged_items}`, `{sa_summary}`. Preserve the exact output format markers `STATUS: NO_UPDATE_NEEDED` and `STATUS: UPDATE_RECOMMENDED`.

### 3. Rewrite `src/policy_factory/prompts/heartbeat/sa-update.md`

Replace "tech policy analysis system" with general policy framing. The existing structure is sound (triage assessment input, file tool usage for updates, feedback memo incorporation). The critical change:

- Add explicit anti-slop writing directive to the "Important" section. The current instruction "Be factual. Stick to what the evidence supports." is insufficient — it doesn't counteract the model's tendency to wrap facts in bureaucratic language. Replace with: the SA update should read like a sharp intelligence briefing, not a corporate memo. No restating the obvious. No "this represents a significant development." No "in the context of Finland's broader strategic landscape." State what changed, what it means for Finland, and what the implications are — in plain, direct language.
- The writing standard for SA updates is: if a sentence could appear in a government press release, delete it and say what you actually mean.

Preserve template variables `{triage_assessment}`, `{sa_content}`, `{feedback_memos}`. Preserve file tool instructions. The SA update agent always escalates on success (Tier 3 → Tier 4), so there is no escalation marker in this prompt.

### 4. Rewrite `src/policy_factory/prompts/classifier/classifier.md`

Replace "tech policy analysis system" with general policy framing. The five-layer descriptions are mostly domain-agnostic already but need the tech-policy framing removed from the header. The classification guidelines section needs scope broadening:

- The current examples are generic enough to work ("News or factual updates → situational-awareness," "Specific policy proposals → policies"). Keep these.
- Add examples covering the full policy domain scope: immigration events, defense developments, economic policy proposals, social welfare changes, EU relations, etc.

Preserve template variables `{layer_summaries}`, `{user_input}`. Preserve the exact output format (`PRIMARY_LAYER`, `SECONDARY_LAYERS`, `CONFIDENCE`, `EXPLANATION`).
