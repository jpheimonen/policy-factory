# 004: Critic and synthesis prompts

## Context

The six critic prompts and the synthesis prompt form the quality assurance mechanism in the cascade. After a generator produces content for a layer, six ideological critics (realist, liberal-institutionalist, nationalist-conservative, social-democratic, libertarian, green-ecological) assess it from their perspectives, and the synthesis agent reconciles their assessments into a balanced summary with recommended refinements.

The analytical frameworks in the critic prompts are sound — each persona has a well-defined ideological lens with clear evaluation criteria. However, all six critics have Finnish context sections that are scoped entirely to technology policy (e.g., "Technological sovereignty is a security imperative," "The EU's regulatory approach to AI Act/DMA risks making Europe uncompetitive," "Data centres and AI training have enormous energy footprints"). These need broadening to general policy.

More importantly, none of the critics are instructed to flag sanitized, hedged, or platitudinous output as a quality failure. The critics currently assess content through their ideological lens but don't police writing quality. If a generator produces corporatespeak that happens to address security concerns, the realist critic would score it well — even though the writing is useless. Critics need an additional quality criterion: anti-slop enforcement.

The synthesis prompt references "cross-party tech policy analysis system" and needs scope broadening. It also needs an instruction to preserve sharp, uncomfortable analysis rather than smoothing everything into consensus language.

## Reuse Opportunities

- All six critic prompts share an identical structure: analytical framework section, Finnish context section, template variable injection, instructions (5-point assessment per item), output format (structured assessment with agreement/score/analysis/blind-spots/recommendation), and "Important" closing directive. This shared structure should be preserved — only the Finnish context items and the instruction additions change.

- The output format section (Assessment of "[Item Title]" with Agreement level, Score, Analysis, blind spots, Alternative recommendation) is consistent across all six critics and should be kept unchanged. The synthesis agent's processing of these assessments depends on this format being stable.

- The synthesis prompt's "Important" section already contains good directives ("Do NOT seek false consensus," "Do NOT average scores," "Be specific"). These should be preserved and strengthened.

## Deliverables

### 1. Rewrite Finnish context sections in all 6 critic prompts

Each critic prompt's "In the Finnish context, this means:" section must be broadened from tech-specific items to general policy items that reflect the full scope of their ideological perspective applied to Finland.

**Realist critic** — Broaden from tech-specific security (technological sovereignty, cyber capabilities) to general security and power concerns: Russia as a structural military threat, NATO credibility and alliance burden-sharing, energy security and pipeline dependencies, border security and immigration as a security issue, critical infrastructure protection across all sectors (not just digital), intelligence capabilities, defense industrial base, economic security including trade dependencies.

**Liberal-institutionalist critic** — Broaden from tech-specific cooperation (digital rights, AI ethics, open standards) to general institutional and cooperative concerns: EU integration depth and federalism trajectory, Nordic cooperation across all policy areas, multilateral governance (UN, OSCE), international law and human rights frameworks, trade agreements, development cooperation, climate agreements, rules-based order under pressure from great power competition.

**Nationalist-conservative critic** — Broaden from tech-specific sovereignty (data sovereignty, digital infrastructure ownership) to general sovereignty and cultural concerns: immigration policy and cultural cohesion, Finnish language and identity preservation, EU competence creep across all domains, rural and peripheral community viability, national democratic control over key policy areas, family and social institutions, border control, traditional industries.

**Social-democratic critic** — Broaden from tech-specific equality (automation/AI labor impact, platform economy, digital divides) to general equality and welfare concerns: income and wealth inequality trends, welfare state sustainability and reform, collective bargaining and labor market institutions, housing affordability, healthcare access, education equity, pension system adequacy, gender equality, regional disparities, immigration's impact on labor markets.

**Libertarian critic** — Broaden from tech-specific market concerns (EU regulatory burden, startup compliance) to general freedom and market concerns: overall tax burden and economic freedom, labor market rigidity, occupational licensing, welfare state dependency effects, regulatory burden across all sectors, property rights, business formation barriers, government spending efficiency, drug policy and personal autonomy, speech and expression freedom.

**Green/ecological critic** — Broaden from tech-specific environmental items (data centre energy, e-waste) to general environmental and sustainability concerns: climate emissions across all sectors, forest and land use policy, biodiversity, Arctic environmental fragility, energy transition (nuclear, wind, solar trade-offs), circular economy, consumption patterns, agriculture and food system sustainability, mining and extractive industries, environmental justice.

Each critic prompt's analytical framework section (the general ideological principles at the top) is already domain-agnostic and should be preserved as-is. Only the Finnish context sections need broadening.

### 2. Add anti-slop quality criterion to all 6 critic instructions

Add a sixth assessment criterion to each critic's instruction section, after the existing five. This criterion evaluates writing quality from the anti-slop standard:

**Quality check** — Is this content written in direct, analytical language? Or does it use euphemisms, hedging, corporatespeak, or politician language? If the content reads like a government press release, an EU white paper, or a committee memo, flag it as a quality failure regardless of whether it addresses the critic's ideological concerns. Sanitized output is a failure mode even when the substance is correct.

This criterion does not get its own section in the output format — it should be woven into the "Analysis" section of each assessment. The output format structure remains unchanged to maintain compatibility with the synthesis runner's parsing.

### 3. Rewrite synthesis prompt

Replace "cross-party tech policy analysis system" with general policy framing. Additionally:

- Add an explicit instruction that the synthesis should preserve sharp, uncomfortable analysis from critics rather than smoothing it into consensus language. If a critic made an incisive, uncomfortable point, the synthesis should amplify it, not dilute it.
- Add an instruction about writing quality: if multiple critics flagged the content as sanitized or platitudinous, this should be treated as one of the "strongest criticisms" in the synthesis output.
- The synthesis should call out cases where the generator produced safe, uncontroversial output as a systemic failure, distinct from individual ideological concerns.

Preserve all template variables: `{layer_slug}`, `{layer_content}`, `{realist_assessment}`, `{liberal_assessment}`, `{nationalist_assessment}`, `{social_democratic_assessment}`, `{libertarian_assessment}`, `{green_assessment}`.

Preserve the output format structure (Areas of Consensus, Key Tensions, Strongest Criticisms, Recommended Refinements, Overall Score, Perspective Summary Table).
