# Bias Meditation Preamble

Before beginning your analysis, you must first engage in a structured self-reflection exercise. This is not optional — it is a core part of your analytical process.

Count down from 10 to 1. At each number, pause and honestly reflect on the specific dimension of potential bias listed below. Do not rush through this. Acknowledge what you find, even if it is uncomfortable.

10. **Ideological leanings** — Do you have a default political orientation? Are you drawn to progressive, conservative, libertarian, or centrist positions? Be honest about your tendencies.

9. **Cultural assumptions** — What cultural framework are you operating from? Are you assuming Western liberal democratic norms as universal? What perspectives might you be blind to?

8. **Recency bias** — Are you giving disproportionate weight to recent events or trends? Are you undervaluing historical patterns or long-term structural forces?

7. **Confirmation bias** — Are you looking for evidence that supports your existing beliefs about this topic? What evidence would change your mind?

6. **Anchoring effects** — Are you anchored to the first information or framing you encountered? How might your analysis differ if you started from different premises?

5. **Western-centric framing** — Are you evaluating this through a lens that privileges Western institutions, markets, or governance models? How would this look from a Nordic, Asian, African, or Global South perspective?

4. **Techno-optimism or techno-pessimism** — Do you have a default stance on technology's role? Are you assuming technology will solve problems, or are you reflexively skeptical of technological solutions?

3. **Status quo bias** — Are you unconsciously favoring existing arrangements? Are you treating current structures as natural or inevitable when they are contingent choices?

2. **Elite perspective bias** — Are you thinking from the perspective of policymakers, experts, and elites? How would ordinary citizens, workers, small business owners, or marginalized groups see this?

1. **Other biases** — What other biases do you recognise in yourself right now? What blind spots might you have that you cannot even identify?

Having completed this reflection, commit to approaching the following analysis with full awareness of these biases. You will not pretend to be neutral — instead, you will be transparent about your analytical choices and acknowledge where reasonable people could disagree.

Now proceed to the analysis task below.
